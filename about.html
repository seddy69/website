About Our Center

Rooted in Faith · Serving the Community · Since 1993

Our Mission

— رسالتنا —

The Tri-State Islamic Center was established to serve as a spiritual home and educational resource for the Muslim community of Dubuque, Iowa and the surrounding tri-state region. We are committed to upholding the teachings of the Quran and the Sunnah of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in everything we do.

Our center strives to be a beacon of faith, a place where every Muslim — regardless of background or ethnicity — can come to worship, learn, and find brotherhood and sisterhood in the community of believers.

We also welcome non-Muslims who wish to learn about Islam in a warm, open, and respectful environment. We believe in building bridges of understanding and cooperation with the wider Dubuque community.

Our Core Values
قيمنا الأساسية
✦
🤲 الإيمان
Faith (Iman)

We are grounded in the belief in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and divine decree.

🕌 العبادة
Worship (Ibadah)

We uphold the five pillars of Islam and strive to make the masjid a living center of sincere devotion to Allah.

📖 العلم
Knowledge (Ilm)

Seeking and sharing knowledge is a sacred duty. We offer classes and resources rooted in the Quran and Sunnah.

🤝 الأخوة
Brotherhood (Ukhuwwah)

The bond between believers transcends ethnicity and nationality. All are equal in the eyes of Allah.

💛 الرحمة
Mercy (Rahmah)

Following the example of the Prophet ﷺ, we extend compassion and care to all people — Muslim and non-Muslim.

🌍 الخدمة
Service (Khidmah)

We are committed to serving our local community, supporting those in need, and contributing to the good of society.

A Diverse Community

— مجتمعنا المتنوع —

One of the greatest blessings of our masjid is the diversity of its congregation. Our community is made up of families and individuals from nearly every part of the Muslim world and beyond, living and worshipping together in Dubuque, Iowa.

This diversity reflects the universal nature of Islam — a faith for all of humanity, regardless of race, language, or origin. In our masjid, Arab and African, South Asian and Southeast Asian, American-born and immigrant, all stand shoulder to shoulder in prayer.
